# Key Insights

## 1. Executive Overview

The dashboard shows that the bank has a total customer base of 166 customers. Savings deposits reached 194.5 billion VND, while the mobilization achievement ratio reached 117.74%, indicating that actual savings mobilization exceeded the planned target.

However, net flow is negative at -3.66 billion VND, which means total outflow is higher than total inflow during the selected period. This requires further analysis in the CASA transaction page.

## 2. Customer Analysis

Customer distribution is concentrated in several major branches. The top branches have a much higher number of customers than the remaining branches, indicating uneven customer coverage.

The customer base includes both individual and corporate customers. Industry distribution also shows that customers are not evenly spread across all industries, with some industries contributing a larger share of customers.

## 3. CASA Transaction Analysis

CASA transactions fluctuate by month. Some months show stronger inflow, while other months show higher outflow. The negative net flow in the overview page suggests that payment transaction behavior should be monitored more closely.

Interbank transaction ratio is useful for understanding how much transaction activity goes outside the bank’s internal system.

## 4. Savings & Mobilization Plan

Savings deposits reached 194.5 billion VND, and the mobilization achievement ratio was above 100%. This indicates that the bank successfully exceeded its mobilization plan.

Savings analysis by term group helps identify whether deposits are mainly concentrated in short-term, medium-term, or long-term products.

## 5. Credit & Repayment Analysis

Outstanding principal is one of the largest risk-related figures in the dashboard. The Credit & Repayment page helps monitor total disbursement, principal repaid, interest collected, and overdue loan proxy.

Some loan contracts may still have outstanding principal after maturity, which should be reviewed as potential overdue exposure.

## 6. Collateral & Risk Exposure

Collateral coverage ratio is used to evaluate whether outstanding loans are sufficiently covered by collateral value. A low coverage ratio indicates that collateral value may not fully protect the bank against credit exposure.

The dashboard supports further investigation by branch, collateral type, and individual loan contract.
